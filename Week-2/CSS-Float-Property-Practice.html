The History

The state legislature established Salt Lake Community College in 1947 as a state-supported vocational school under the management and control of the Utah State Board for Vocational Education. Classes began in the fall of 1948. In 1978 it became part of the Utah System of Higher Education under the jurisdiction of the Utah State Board of Regents.

Currently it is the state's largest two-year college with the most diverse student body. It serves more than 60,000 students on 10 campuses as well as through online classes.
